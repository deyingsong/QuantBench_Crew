# O'Hara Market Microstructure Theory

Source: Maureen O'Hara, *Market Microstructure Theory*.

## Why This Source Matters

O'Hara provides the conceptual foundations for interpreting spreads, quote
changes, order flow, and market design. The central lesson is that observed
prices depend on the trading mechanism and on what intermediaries infer from
orders. Use this source to identify the economic mechanism a paper claims to
measure.

## Core Lenses

### Inventory

Dealers supply immediacy while facing stochastic buy and sell flows. Inventory
can threaten dealer viability, impose portfolio risk, and shift quotes toward
a preferred position. Predictions depend on assumptions about risk aversion,
competition, hedging access, order arrivals, and inventory bounds.

### Information And Adverse Selection

Trades can reveal private information. In sequential-trade models, a buy raises
the market maker's estimate of value because it may come from an informed
buyer; a sell does the reverse. Competitive dealers recover expected losses to
informed traders from uninformed flow through the spread.

### Strategic Trading

Informed traders choose timing and size while anticipating price impact.
Noise or liquidity flow provides camouflage. Prices reveal information
gradually because strategic traders do not immediately expose their entire
signal.

### Market Design

Call auctions, continuous auctions, dealers, specialists, and multimarket
structures aggregate information differently. A result derived for one
mechanism should not be assumed to hold under another.

## Assumptions To Identify

For every microstructure model, state:

- who is informed and what they know;
- who supplies liquidity and whether they compete;
- whether orders are unit-sized or size is strategic;
- whether trading is sequential, batched, or continuous;
- whether dealers observe identities, order flow, or inventory;
- whether intermediaries are risk neutral and can hedge;
- how public information arrives;
- what ends the game and determines fundamental value.

These choices are not decoration. They determine whether spread, depth, and
price impact are attributed to inventory, information, monopoly rents, or
order-processing costs.

## Reviewer Diagnostics

- Do the empirical proxies uniquely identify the claimed mechanism?
- Could permanent-looking impact come from public news or correlated flow?
- Could transitory impact reflect inventory, bid-ask bounce, or mechanical
  price pressure?
- Is a modern anonymous LOB being interpreted with a single-dealer model
  without explaining the mapping?
- Does the paper confuse liquidity provision with uninformed trading, or
  liquidity taking with informed trading?
- Are welfare claims explicit about gains from trade, information rents, and
  intermediary profits?

## Reviewer Use

Use O'Hara as the first-pass taxonomy. Before inspecting an estimator or
strategy, ask what friction creates the result. If a paper cannot articulate
the mechanism and the market institution that supports it, its causal story is
weak even when its prediction is statistically accurate.
