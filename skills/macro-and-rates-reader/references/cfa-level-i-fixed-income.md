# CFA Level I Fixed Income

Source: Kaplan Schweser, *2023 Level I CFA Book 4: Fixed Income*.

## Why This Source Matters

This source provides a disciplined baseline taxonomy for fixed-income
instruments, yield measures, securitization, interest-rate risk, credit risk,
and derivatives. Use it to check definitions, units, and instrument mechanics
before accepting a sophisticated macro or rates claim.

## Instrument And Cash-Flow Scope

Fixed-income securities differ by:

- issuer and source of repayment;
- seniority, collateral, and covenants;
- coupon structure and maturity;
- currency and indexation;
- embedded calls, puts, conversions, or credit links;
- securitization and tranche priority.

A macro view does not affect every bond uniformly. Contract features can
dominate the response to rates, spreads, or inflation.

## Yield Measures And Curves

Distinguish:

- yield to maturity and holding-period return;
- spot, coupon-yield, par, and forward curves;
- benchmark or G-spread;
- swap or I-spread;
- Z-spread;
- option-adjusted spread.

Yield to maturity is a summary under reinvestment and hold-to-maturity
assumptions, not a promised realized return. G- and I-spreads can be misleading
when the curve is not flat. OAS depends on the interest-rate and option model.

## Interest-Rate Risk

Use the correct sensitivity:

- Macaulay duration for timing of cash flows;
- modified duration for yield sensitivity of option-free bonds;
- effective duration for bonds with embedded options;
- key-rate duration for curve-shape risk;
- money duration or PVBP for dollar exposure;
- convexity for second-order effects.

Portfolio duration assumes a particular mapping from market-rate changes to
security yields. It can conceal nonparallel curve, spread, and optionality
risk.

## Sources Of Return And Risk

Bond return can arise from:

- coupon income and reinvestment;
- price change from rates;
- spread change;
- carry and roll-down;
- currency movement;
- default and recovery;
- embedded-option exercise;
- liquidity change.

Require a paper to attribute return rather than label all bond performance a
rates effect.

## Credit And Liquidity

Credit risk includes default probability and loss severity, but ratings and
spreads are imperfect summaries. Credit quality should consider capacity,
collateral, covenants, and character, while sovereign and high-yield analysis
require additional context.

Spread movements can reflect both credit and liquidity. Repo rollover,
commercial-paper rollover, and stressed-market liquidity are distinct risks
from mark-to-market duration.

## Securitization And Embedded Options

Mortgage and asset-backed securities redistribute cash-flow, prepayment,
extension, and credit risks through structure and tranche priority. Prepayment
changes with rates and borrower behavior, creating path-dependent duration and
convexity.

Do not apply option-free bond analytics mechanically to callable, putable,
mortgage, or structured instruments.

## Reviewer Diagnostics

- Are quoted yields comparable in convention, periodicity, currency, and
  maturity?
- Is the paper using the correct curve and spread measure?
- Does duration match the instrument's optionality?
- Are macro, credit, liquidity, and option components separated?
- Are reinvestment and holding-period assumptions explicit?
- Does a sovereign bond's currency and repayment capacity match the paper's
  "risk-free" assumption?

## Reviewer Use

Use this source as the definitions-and-mechanics check. It is especially useful
when a paper's econometrics are complex but its yield, duration, spread, or
instrument interpretation may be wrong.
