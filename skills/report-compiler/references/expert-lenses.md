# Expert Diagnostic Lenses

Use these lenses as independent questions. Do not imitate personalities, invent
quotes, or treat reputation as evidence.

## Statistical Honesty

### Marcos Lopez de Prado

- Were all trials disclosed?
- Does performance survive multiple-testing correction?
- Are cross-validation, embargoes, and purging appropriate for dependent data?
- Is the result likely selected from a large search?

### Victor Niederhoffer

- What market maxim is being assumed rather than tested?
- What observation would falsify the thesis?
- Are hidden tails or rare reversals masked by averages?

### Harry Markowitz And William Sharpe

- Does the strategy improve the portfolio after covariance and factor exposure?
- Is alpha distinct from compensation for known risk?
- Are risk and return measured consistently?

## Edge And Research Process

### Jim Simons, David Shaw, Igor Tulchinsky, And WorldQuant

- Can the research process produce many independent signals rather than one
  fragile discovery?
- Are data quality, experiment tracking, and repeatability institutionalized?
- Does the edge survive combination, scaling, and continued monitoring?

### Richard Craib And Numerai

- Are model diagnostics, feature exposures, and ensemble contribution measured?
- Do incentives reward honest out-of-sample contribution?
- Does neutralization remove unintended bets without removing the claimed edge?

### Jane Street

- Can another expert inspect, challenge, and maintain the implementation?
- Are edge cases, execution details, and code-review concerns explicit?
- Is the result robust enough for adversarial operational scrutiny?

### Ernest Chan

- Are timestamps, lags, costs, fills, and capacity realistic?
- Is the backtest implementable rather than merely descriptive?
- Does the effect persist out of sample?

## Economic Coherence And Alternatives

### Cliff Asness

- Is there a coherent reason the premium should exist and persist?
- Can the thesis survive long painful periods without being rewritten?
- Does it beat a simple factor or portfolio alternative?

### John Bogle

- Does complexity earn its fees, turnover, taxes, and governance burden?
- Is a low-cost passive alternative the stronger baseline?

### David Siegel

- Is the evidence durable across long horizons and valuation conditions?
- Is the interpretation overly dependent on one historical context?

## Risk, Sizing, And Regime

### Ed Thorp

- What is the expected value after uncertainty and costs?
- How should sizing change under estimation error?
- What is the risk of ruin?

### Ray Dalio

- Which economic regimes support or break the strategy?
- Is apparent diversification real under stress?
- Which causal machine is the thesis relying on?

### Stanley Druckenmiller And Paul Tudor Jones

- Is the payoff asymmetric?
- What happens when the regime, liquidity, or policy backdrop changes?
- Is capital preserved when the thesis is wrong?

### Larry Fink

- How do liquidity, flows, policy, and market structure affect practical scale?
- What institutional constraints change the result?

## Variant Perception, Catalysts, And Accountability

### Bill Ackman, Dan Loeb, Carl Icahn, Julian Robertson, And Chase Coleman

- What does the paper know or interpret differently from the market?
- What catalyst or mechanism realizes the value?
- What evidence invalidates the thesis and triggers exit?
- Are management, incentives, or governance central to the result?

## Institutional Controls

### Ken Griffin, Steve Cohen, Israel Englander, And Morgan Slade

- Is the edge supported by repeatable process, risk controls, and independent
  challenge?
- Can exposures and failures be identified before they become existential?
- Does the organization learn from mistakes and control concentration?

### Christina Qi And Dimitri Bianco

- Are model monitoring, limitations, ownership, and escalation explicit?
- Does the team distinguish model performance from model-risk governance?
- Are culture and incentives aligned with honest reporting?

### Governance Failure Caution

Material involving Sam Bankman-Fried is used only as a negative control:

- polished narratives and apparent profitability do not replace segregation,
  controls, auditability, or conflict management
- incentives can corrupt reported success
- governance failure can dominate model quality

Do not cite this as a positive investing lens.

## Using The Lenses

For each lens:

1. Ask the relevant questions.
2. Cite available evidence.
3. Label missing evidence.
4. State whether the lens supports, challenges, or cannot assess the thesis.
5. Preserve disagreements among lenses.
