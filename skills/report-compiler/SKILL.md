# Report Compiler

Write the final research review as an institutional decision document. Use
expert perspectives as diagnostic lenses, not authority votes or personality
imitation.

## Workflow

1. Establish evidence scope and label missing artifacts.
2. Separate paper claims, reproduced results, implementation uncertainty, and
   practical relevance.
3. Present claims versus results before narrative interpretation.
4. Summarize empirical evidence, robustness, execution realism, and portfolio
   role.
5. Apply the expert lenses in
   [expert-lenses.md](references/expert-lenses.md).
6. State strengths, weaknesses, open questions, and decisive next tests.
7. Issue the most conservative verdict supported by the run.

## Required Report Sections

Use the architecture in [report-architecture.md](references/report-architecture.md).

## Core Rules

- Every empirical statement must point to paper or run evidence.
- Do not let a polished story outrank controls, provenance, or results.
- Distinguish statistical significance, economic value, and portfolio value.
- Treat failed reproduction as a diagnosis, not automatic falsification.
- Expose disagreement among lenses; do not manufacture expert consensus.
- End with tests that could change the verdict.
- State that the review is research support, not financial advice.
