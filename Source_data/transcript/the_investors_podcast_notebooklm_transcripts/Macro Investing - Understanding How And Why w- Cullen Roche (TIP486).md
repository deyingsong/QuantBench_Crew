## Transcript

This interview features author and former professional poker player Annie Duke, who discusses the psychological complexities of decision-making and the importance of knowing when to quit. She highlights how her academic background in cognitive psychology and her experience in high-stakes poker both illustrate the necessity of closing feedback loops under conditions of uncertainty. Duke introduces the concept of "kill criteria," which are pre-established benchmarks designed to help individuals overcome cognitive hurdles like sunk cost effect and loss aversion. By making forecasts explicit and using a "quitting coach," people can better navigate the biases that often tie their identity to failing endeavors. The discussion emphasizes that walking away is a strategic tool that preserves resources for more valuable opportunities in both investing and life.
